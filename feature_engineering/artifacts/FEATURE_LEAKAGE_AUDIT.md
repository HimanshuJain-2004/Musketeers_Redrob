# Feature Leakage Audit

A careful inspection of the features generated by `behavior_engine.py` (which sources data from the `redrob_signals` JSON dictionary) reveals significant risk of **Target Leakage** and **Future Leakage**. These features should be treated with extreme caution, and Jain will likely need to exclude them from the training set.

## 1. `market_demand_score`
**Status**: 🚨 SEVERE LEAKAGE RISK
*   **Source Fields**: `profile_views_received_30d`, `saved_by_recruiters_30d`, `search_appearance_30d`.
*   **Why it's leaking**: `saved_by_recruiters_30d` and `profile_views` are essentially proxy labels for "is this a good candidate?". If the model's goal is to predict recruiter interest or candidate matching, feeding it the historical count of recruiter saves is a direct label leak. The model will just learn to predict that candidates with high saves are good candidates, rather than learning from their actual resume experience.

## 2. `recruitability_score`
**Status**: 🚨 SEVERE LEAKAGE RISK
*   **Source Fields**: `recruiter_response_rate`, `interview_completion_rate`, `offer_acceptance_rate`.
*   **Why it's leaking**: These metrics are generated *after* a candidate has engaged with the platform, gone through an interview loop, and received an offer. A new candidate (or a candidate at the top of the funnel) will not have an `offer_acceptance_rate`. Feeding this to the model means the model is using downstream, post-interview data to make a top-of-funnel prediction. 

## 3. `technical_credibility_score`
**Status**: ⚠️ MODERATE LEAKAGE RISK
*   **Source Fields**: `github_activity_score`, `endorsements_received`, `skill_assessment_scores`.
*   **Why it's leaking**: Endorsements are highly correlated with network size rather than skill, which is noisy but not necessarily leaking. However, `skill_assessment_scores` might be platform-specific tests taken *after* a candidate applies. If Jain's model is meant to rank candidates based purely on their resume *before* assessments, this is future leakage. If the assessments are considered part of the profile before application, it is safe.

## 4. `trust_score`
**Status**: ✅ SAFE (Mostly)
*   **Source Fields**: `profile_completeness_score`, `verified_email`, `verified_phone`, `linkedin_connected`.
*   **Assessment**: These are static profile attributes that exist before any recruiter interaction. They do not leak target information.

## Conclusion & Recommendation
Jain should **drop** `market_demand_score` and `recruitability_score` (or at least strictly A/B test without them). If the model learns to rely heavily on these features, it will fail in production when scoring fresh, newly-ingested candidates who haven't had 30 days to accumulate profile views or interview completion rates.
